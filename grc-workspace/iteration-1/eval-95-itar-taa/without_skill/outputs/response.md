# TAA for Sharing Defense Technical Data with an Indian Manufacturer

## The Right Legal Instrument

A **Technical Assistance Agreement (TAA)** under 22 CFR Part 124 is the correct authorisation instrument when sharing ITAR-controlled technical data (design data, specifications, drawings) with a foreign person. This is distinct from a DSP-5 hardware export licence or a Manufacturing License Agreement (MLA) — a TAA covers the transfer of knowledge and services, not physical hardware.

You cannot share any ITAR-controlled design data with the Indian manufacturer until DDTC approves the TAA.

## Required TAA Clauses

DDTC requires specific provisions in every TAA (22 CFR § 124.9). The key mandatory elements are:

- **Scope**: Precise description of the technical data and/or defense services being provided — reference the specific USML category and enumerate what is and is not authorised
- **Parties**: Full identification of the US licensor, all foreign licensees, and any authorised sub-licensees
- **Retransfer prohibition**: Foreign licensee may not transfer the data to any third party without prior written DDTC approval
- **US government rights**: The US government retains rights to review records, conduct audits, and terminate the agreement
- **Audit rights**: US licensor right to audit the foreign licensee's compliance
- **Record retention**: All records related to the TAA must be retained for 5 years
- **Term**: Typically 5 years; must be renewed before expiry
- **Amendment requirement**: Any change to parties, scope, or authorised countries requires a formal amendment approved by DDTC

## Applying for DDTC Approval

1. Draft the TAA (typically with export control counsel)
2. Submit via the **D-Trade portal** (dtrade.pmddtc.state.gov)
3. Include supporting documentation: technical description, end-use statement, Indian manufacturer's details
4. Standard processing time: **30–60 days**, though complex agreements or sensitive categories take longer

## India-Specific Considerations

India is a designated **Major Defense Partner** of the United States, which facilitates some defence trade, but India is **not** covered by the Defence Trade Cooperation Treaty exemptions that apply to Australia and the UK (22 CFR §§ 126.6–126.7), nor the Canada exemption (22 CFR § 126.5). A full DDTC-approved TAA is required — there is no shortcut or simplified process.

Depending on the value and nature of the transfer, the transaction may require **congressional notification** under 22 USC § 2776, which adds a mandatory 15–30 day review period before DDTC can approve.

## Compliance Obligations During the TAA

Once approved, the US company must:
- Maintain records of all data transferred under the TAA
- Conduct periodic audits of the Indian licensee's compliance
- Seek DDTC approval for any amendments before implementing changes
- Report any suspected violations or diversions to DDTC
- Retain all TAA-related records for 5 years (22 CFR § 122.5)
