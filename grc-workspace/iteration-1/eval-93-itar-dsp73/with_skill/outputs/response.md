# ITAR Temporary Export License for Night-Vision Rifle Scope — European Trade Show Circuit

## Classification Confirmation: USML Category XII

A night-vision rifle scope is explicitly controlled under **USML Category XII — Fire Control, Range Finder, Optical and Guidance and Control Equipment** (22 CFR § 121.1, Category XII). This category covers military targeting optics, infrared imaging (military), and night-vision devices (NVDs). There is no ambiguity here — no Commodity Jurisdiction (CJ) determination is needed. The item is a defense article subject to the full requirements of the International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), 22 CFR Parts 120–130.

---

## Required License: DSP-73 (Temporary Export)

Because the night-vision scope is leaving the US **temporarily** for trade show display and is intended to **return** to the United States, the correct license is the **DSP-73 (Temporary Export License)** under **22 CFR § 123.5**.

A DSP-5 (permanent export) is not appropriate because there is no transfer of ownership or permanent sale to the UK distributor. A Warehouse/Distribution Agreement (22 CFR § 124.14) would apply only if you were stocking items abroad for resale — which is not the case here.

---

## Application Process — D-Trade Portal

All DSP-73 applications are submitted electronically through **DDTC's D-Trade portal** (https://dtrade.pmddtc.state.gov). The following information is required:

1. **Applicant information**: Your DDTC registration number (confirm registration is current under 22 CFR § 122.1).
2. **Item description**: Precise description of the night-vision rifle scope, including USML Category XII citation, model number(s), quantity, and unit value.
3. **Serial numbers**: Document all serial numbers for each unit being exported — this is critical for temporary export tracking and return verification.
4. **Purpose statement**: State clearly that the purpose is "trade show display" and the item will be returned to the US.
5. **Duration**: Specify the 6-month trade show circuit as the requested license period. The license expiry date must be set to allow sufficient time for the items to return to the US.
6. **Destinations**: List all countries in the European show circuit (e.g., UK, Germany, France, etc.), as each destination country must be identified.
7. **UK distributor details**: Identify the UK distributor as the foreign consignee/agent handling the items.

**Processing time**: Allow 30–60 days for DDTC review. Submit well in advance of your planned shipment date.

---

## Key License Conditions and Restrictions

### 1. No Transfer to Foreign Ownership
The DSP-73 strictly prohibits the transfer of the items to foreign ownership, possession, or control on a permanent basis. The UK distributor is acting as a custodian/agent — title and ownership must remain with the US exporter throughout the trade show circuit.

### 2. Items Must Return by License Expiry
All exported items **must physically return to the United States** by the license expiry date. Failure to return the items by the expiry date constitutes a potential ITAR violation under 22 CFR § 127. If delays occur, contact DDTC proactively to seek an amendment or extension before the expiry.

### 3. No Operational or Combat Use
DSP-73 licenses prohibit the use of exported items in combat or operational military deployment. Use is limited to display and demonstration purposes at the trade shows.

### 4. No Sale or Re-Transfer
The items may not be sold, loaned, leased, or otherwise transferred to any third party during the trade show circuit without separate DDTC authorisation.

---

## Re-Export Across European Countries — DDTC Approval Required

This is a critical compliance point for your trade show circuit. When the night-vision scope moves from the UK to other European countries (e.g., Germany, France, Netherlands), each movement constitutes a **re-export** under ITAR.

**Re-export of ITAR-controlled defense articles from one foreign country to another requires prior DDTC approval** (22 CFR § 126.14). The DSP-73 authorises the initial export from the US to the UK, but **does not automatically authorise onward movement to other European countries**.

### How to Address This

There are two approaches:

**Option A — List all destination countries on the DSP-73 at application time**: When submitting the DSP-73, provide a complete itinerary of all European countries in the show circuit and request DDTC authorise movement among all listed countries. DDTC may approve this within the scope of the single DSP-73 if the purpose (trade show display) and all destinations are clearly described.

**Option B — Seek individual re-export authorisation per country**: If the itinerary is not finalised at application time, obtain the DSP-73 for the initial UK export, then seek DDTC's written approval (via amendment or separate correspondence) for each subsequent country leg before the items are moved.

Option A is strongly preferred as it reduces administrative burden and risk of gaps in authorisation coverage.

---

## UK Defence Trade Cooperation Treaty Note

The US–UK Defence Trade Cooperation Treaty provides some streamlined licensing pathways, but it is **not a blanket exemption** for USML Category XII items. Verify with your ITAR counsel whether any treaty-based exemption (22 CFR § 126.7) applies to your specific scope model and transaction structure before relying on it. For a multi-country European circuit, a DSP-73 covering all destinations is the most straightforward and legally certain path.
