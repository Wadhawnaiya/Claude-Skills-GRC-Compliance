# ITAR Export License for Night-Vision Rifle Scope — Trade Show Circuit (UK/Europe)

## Situation Summary

You intend to export a night-vision rifle scope classified under **USML Category XII(b)** (fire control, range finder, optical and guidance and control equipment) to a UK distributor, which will then carry the item through a multi-country European trade show circuit for six months. This export of a defense article requires a license from the U.S. Department of State, Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC).

---

## The Correct License: DSP-73 (Temporary Export License)

Because the item will be exported **temporarily** — for display purposes and will be returned to the United States — the applicable license is the **DSP-73, Application for Temporary Export of Unclassified Defense Articles**.

### Why DSP-73 and Not DSP-5?

| Factor | DSP-5 (Permanent Export) | DSP-73 (Temporary Export) |
|---|---|---|
| Intended return to U.S. | No | Yes |
| Purpose | Sale/transfer of title | Demonstration, trade shows, testing |
| Duration | Permanent | Up to 4 years (extensions possible) |
| ATA Carnet compatible | No | Yes |
| Your scenario | Does not apply | **Applies** |

A DSP-5 (permanent export license) would be required only if title/ownership transfers permanently to the UK distributor. Since the item is coming back after the trade shows, DSP-73 is the correct instrument.

---

## Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1 — Verify USML Classification

Before applying, confirm the exact ECCN/USML category:
- Night-vision rifle scopes with image intensifier tubes typically fall under **USML Category XII(b)** — electro-optical and night-vision equipment.
- If the scope contains a thermal imaging detector (focal plane array), it may additionally implicate **Category XII(d)**.
- Confirm with your company's empowered official or a licensed export counsel that no EAR (Commerce Control List) classification applies instead.

### Step 2 — Register with DDTC (if not already registered)

Your company must be registered as a manufacturer/exporter with DDTC under 22 CFR Part 122.

- **Form:** DS-2032 (Statement of Registration)
- **Fee:** $2,750 per year (as of 2024; confirm current fee)
- **Portal:** D-Trade (https://dtrade.pmddtc.state.gov)
- Registration must be current at the time of application and throughout the license period.

### Step 3 — Gather Required Documentation

Assemble the following before filing in D-Trade:

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **Technical Specification Sheet** | Full description of the item: model number, NSN (if any), key specs (magnification, image intensifier generation, reticle type) |
| **Quantity and Value** | Number of units and fair market value for the items being exported |
| **DSP-83** (Non-Transfer and Use Certificate) | Required for USML items; must be signed by the UK end user (your distributor) and countersigned by the UK government if required |
| **End-Use Statement / Letter from UK Distributor** | On distributor letterhead: confirms the item is for trade show display only, will not be re-exported without U.S. approval, and will be returned by a specified date |
| **Itinerary of Trade Shows** | List of specific events, dates, venues, and countries where the item will be displayed |
| **ATA Carnet** (strongly recommended) | Issued by the U.S. Council for International Business; functions as a "merchandise passport" for temporary exports; required by most European customs authorities and simplifies re-importation |
| **Purchase/Consignment Agreement** | Between your company and the UK distributor, confirming the temporary nature |
| **Export Compliance Program documentation** | DDTC may request evidence of your internal compliance program |

### Step 4 — Complete the DSP-73 Application in D-Trade

Log into the **D-Trade portal** at https://dtrade.pmddtc.state.gov and file electronically:

**Key fields to complete:**

- **Applicant:** Your company (registered exporter)
- **Consignee:** UK distributor (name, address, registration details)
- **End User:** Same as consignee, or list each country's trade show host organization
- **Foreign Countries of Temporary Sojourn:** List every European country where the item will travel — e.g., United Kingdom, Germany, France, Netherlands, Italy, etc. Do not omit any country; unlicensed transit is a violation.
- **USML Category/Subcategory:** Category XII, specify subcategory (b) or as applicable
- **Item Description:** Full technical description matching the specification sheet
- **Quantity:** Number of units
- **Value:** Dollar value
- **Requested Duration:** Up to 4 years permitted; for a 6-month circuit, request 12 months to allow buffer for return shipping and customs delays
- **Purpose:** "Trade show display and demonstration — item to be returned to the United States upon conclusion of events"

**Supporting attachments** (uploaded directly in D-Trade):
- Technical data package
- Signed DSP-83
- End-user letter and trade show itinerary
- Any relevant MOU or agreement

### Step 5 — Pay the Filing Fee

DDTC charges a fee per license application. As of 2025, fees vary by transaction type. Confirm the current fee schedule on the DDTC website. Payment is made through D-Trade.

### Step 6 — DDTC Review

- **Standard processing time:** 30–60 business days for Category XII items (which receive enhanced scrutiny as a sensitive category)
- **Expedited processing:** Available in limited circumstances; contact DDTC directly
- DDTC may issue a **Request for Additional Information (RAI)** — respond promptly to avoid delays
- State Department may coordinate with Department of Defense (DTSA) for technical review
- Plan for **at least 90 days** lead time before the first trade show date

### Step 7 — Obtain the ATA Carnet

Simultaneously with or shortly after filing the DSP-73, apply for an **ATA Carnet** through the U.S. Council for International Business (USCIB):

- Website: https://www.atacarnet.com
- The carnet covers customs duties in participating countries and simplifies border crossings
- All European Union countries and the UK are ATA Carnet contracting parties
- The carnet must list each country of sojourn matching the DSP-73
- Carnet validity: typically 1 year; must cover the entire circuit duration

### Step 8 — Shipper's Export Declaration / Electronic Export Information (EEI)

For each physical export shipment:
- File **Electronic Export Information (EEI)** in the Automated Export System (AES) via ACE (https://ace.cbp.dhs.gov)
- Reference the DSP-73 license number and USML Category XII classification
- Use Schedule B code appropriate for the item
- File before the item departs U.S. territory

### Step 9 — Country-Specific Considerations for Europe

Post-Brexit, the UK is no longer part of the EU's common trade regime. This affects the circuit:

| Country/Bloc | Consideration |
|---|---|
| **United Kingdom** | UK has its own export control regime (OGEL/SIEL under the Export Control Order 2008). Your UK distributor must verify they hold the appropriate UK import authorization and any re-export licenses required to move the item into EU countries. |
| **EU Countries (Germany, France, etc.)** | EU Common Position on arms exports; each country may have national import authorization requirements for USML defense articles. Verify with each host country's customs authority. |
| **Germany** | BAFA (Federal Office for Economic Affairs and Export Control) controls imports of defense equipment — your UK distributor may need a German import permit |
| **France** | SBDU/DGA controls — temporary import under ATA Carnet typically accepted for trade shows but confirm with French customs |

The DSP-73 covers the U.S. export side. The UK distributor bears responsibility for compliance with local import/re-export laws in each country visited. Contractually require your distributor to obtain all necessary host-country permits before crossing each border.

## Additional ITAR Obligations During the Circuit

### Re-Export Prohibition
The UK distributor **may not re-export** the item to any third country not listed on the DSP-73 without obtaining a separate U.S. re-export authorization (or license amendment). If the show circuit expands to additional countries, you must amend the license before the item travels there.

### No Technology Transfer
Trade show demonstration must be conducted in a manner that prevents unauthorized nationals from accessing technical data. Under ITAR 22 CFR § 120.50 (release of technical data to foreign persons), even showing a foreign national how the scope operates could constitute a "deemed export" of ITAR-controlled technical data if that data is not already in the public domain. Work with your distributor to:
- Restrict hands-on access by nationals of embargoed countries (Iran, North Korea, Russia, China, etc.)
- Avoid providing manuals, schematics, or maintenance data to foreign persons without a separate authorization

### Recordkeeping
Under 22 CFR § 122.5, you must maintain records of the transaction for **5 years** from the date of export, including the DSP-73, ATA Carnet, shipping records, EEI filings, and all correspondence with the distributor.

### Return of the Item
Upon conclusion of the circuit:
- The item must be returned to the United States
- File EEI for the re-importation (CBP entry)
- Retain documentation proving return
- If the item will instead be sold to the UK distributor or another foreign party, you must obtain a separate **DSP-5 permanent export license** before transfer of title occurs

## Potential Exemptions to Consider (and Why They Likely Don't Apply)

### 22 CFR § 123.4 — License Exemptions for Defense Articles
Certain temporary export exemptions exist (e.g., for items accompanying U.S. government personnel, NATO cooperative programs), but none cleanly cover a commercial trade show circuit through multiple non-NATO-only countries handled by a foreign distributor. **A DSP-73 license is required.**

### 22 CFR § 123.22(b)(2) — Trade Show Exemption
ITAR does contain a limited trade show exemption for items exported by U.S. persons to U.S.-controlled exhibitions. However, because this item will be handled by a **UK distributor** (a foreign person), not a U.S. person, and will travel across multiple countries over 6 months, this exemption does not apply. A DSP-73 is required.
