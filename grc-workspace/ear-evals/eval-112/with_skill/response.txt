DEEMED EXPORT ANALYSIS — CHINESE NATIONAL EMPLOYEE ACCESS TO 5D002 SOURCE CODE
================================================================================
Prepared under: Export Administration Regulations (EAR), 15 CFR Parts 730–774
Administered by: Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), U.S. Department of Commerce

SCENARIO
--------
- Company: US software company
- Employee: Chinese national, Chinese/Australian dual citizen, located in the US
- Item at issue: Source code for a product classified under ECCN 5D002
- Question: What deemed export obligations apply? Is a licence required?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. ECCN CLASSIFICATION — 5D002
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ECCN 5D002 covers software specifically designed or modified for the development,
production, or use of commodities or software controlled under ECCN 5A002 or 5B002
(information security equipment), as well as software using "cryptography" as defined
in the CCL. In plain terms, 5D002 captures encryption software — including source
code for encryption products.

Reasons for Control under 5D002:
  - EI  (Encryption Items) — primary control
  - NS  (National Security)
  - AT  (Anti-Terrorism)

This is a controlled software classification, not EAR99. The source code of your
encryption product is almost certainly 5D002 software technology or software itself.
Note that "technology" for the development of 5D002 software (e.g., design
documentation, architecture specs, cryptographic algorithms) would be classified
5E002. For this analysis, we address source code access, which constitutes release
of the 5D002 software itself.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DEEMED EXPORT RULE — § 734.13
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Under 15 CFR § 734.13, the "release" of controlled technology or software to a
foreign national in the United States is deemed to be an export to that person's
country of nationality. This is the cornerstone of deemed export compliance.

"Release" is defined broadly and includes:
  - Visual inspection of controlled items or materials
  - Oral briefings and demonstrations
  - Electronic transmission (email, file share, repository access)
  - Hands-on access to controlled systems or code

Granting your employee access to the 5D002 source code repository — whether via
GitHub, GitLab, an internal code server, or any other means — constitutes a
"release" of controlled software under § 734.13. This triggers a deemed export
analysis immediately.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. DUAL NATIONALITY RULE — MOST RESTRICTIVE NATIONALITY APPLIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the critical issue in your scenario. BIS applies the "most restrictive
nationality" rule to individuals holding dual or multiple citizenships:

  "For purposes of the deemed export rule, BIS will look to the most
   restrictive licensing treatment applicable to any nationality held
   by the individual."

Your employee holds both Chinese and Australian citizenship. The analysis is:

  - Australia: Country Group A:5 (allied/partner nation); highly favourable
    licensing treatment; many licence exceptions available
  - China: Country Group D:1 (national security-controlled country); D:5
    (arms embargo); significantly more restrictive licensing treatment

Result: The employee is treated as a CHINESE NATIONAL for all deemed export
licensing purposes, regardless of Australian citizenship.

Australian dual citizenship provides NO relief. The Chinese nationality controls.

This rule is not discretionary. It is BIS policy applied consistently across
enforcement actions. You may not argue that the employee's Australian passport,
Australian residency, or Australian cultural ties change the nationality
determination.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. LICENCE REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS FOR 5D002 TO CHINA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1 — Country Chart Check (15 CFR Part 738, Supplement No. 1)

For ECCN 5D002, the applicable Reasons for Control are EI, NS, and AT.
Check each against China (D:1 country) on the Commerce Country Chart:

  Control Column         China
  -----------------------------------------------
  NS Column 1            X  (licence required)
  NS Column 2            X  (licence required)
  EI (Encryption Items)  Special — see ENC analysis below
  AT Column 1            X  (licence required)

A licence requirement is triggered under NS controls. The EI control for 5D002
operates slightly differently and is addressed through the ENC licence exception
framework below.

Step 2 — Can a Licence Exception Apply?

Review the key licence exceptions under Part 740:

  TSU (Technology and Software Unrestricted, § 740.13):
  - Covers published software and technology in the public domain
  - Does NOT cover proprietary source code that is not publicly available
  - Your source code is NOT publicly released → TSU does not apply

  ENC (Encryption, § 740.17):
  - The ENC exception is the primary pathway for 5D002 items
  - Allows export/deemed export of certain encryption software to most destinations
    after a one-time review and annual reporting
  - Key eligibility tiers:
      § 740.17(b)(1): Mass-market encryption products — broadest availability,
        including to most Country Group D:1 countries, after one-time BIS/NSA
        review notification
      § 740.17(b)(2)-(3): Non-standard, proprietary encryption items — more
        restricted; generally NOT available to Country Group D:1 nations including
        China for source code

  CRITICAL: ENC § 740.17(b)(1) for mass-market products EXCLUDES source code.
  ENC § 740.17(b)(4) specifically restricts availability for encryption source
  code and non-publicly available encryption software to Country Group D:1
  countries including China.

  For 5D002 source code released as a deemed export to a Chinese national:
  - If the source code is for a product that qualifies as "mass market"
    (widely available, off-the-shelf, with no specialised military/government
    features), review § 740.17(b)(1) carefully — but source code (as opposed
    to object code) may still be excluded
  - For proprietary or non-mass-market encryption source code, ENC is generally
    NOT available for deemed exports to Chinese nationals

  CIV (Civil End-Users, § 740.7):
  - Available for NS-controlled items to D:1 countries for civil end-use
  - Does NOT apply to EI-controlled items (5D002 has EI control)
  - CIV is not available here

  GBS, TSR, LVS: Not applicable to 5D002 technology/software deemed exports
  to D:1 countries.

CONCLUSION ON LICENCE EXCEPTIONS:
For most 5D002 source code deemed export scenarios involving Chinese nationals,
no licence exception will be available. A BIS export licence will be required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. IS A LICENCE REQUIRED? — BOTTOM LINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

YES. A deemed export licence is required before your company allows this employee
access to the 5D002 source code, unless a specific exception analysis confirms
eligibility.

The specific pathway is:
  - Apply for a deemed export licence via SNAP-R (snap-r.bis.doc.gov)
  - Use Form BIS-748P (Multipurpose Application Form)
  - Describe the technology (5D002 source code), the nationality of the employee
    (Chinese national), and the intended use (software development within the
    company)
  - BIS/NSA will review; encryption licence applications involve NSA referral
  - Review timeline: up to 90 days; plan accordingly

IMPORTANT: Do NOT grant the employee access to the source code while the licence
application is pending. Doing so would constitute an unlicensed deemed export —
a violation carrying civil penalties up to $374,474 per violation (or 2x the
transaction value) and potential criminal exposure under § 764.2.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. POSSIBLE EXCEPTION — FUNDAMENTAL RESEARCH (§ 734.8)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If any of the software development work qualifies as "fundamental research" —
basic or applied research at an accredited institution where results are ordinarily
published — the Fundamental Research Exclusion (FRE) under § 734.8 may apply.

However, for a US software company developing a commercial encryption product:
  - The work is not conducted at an accredited institution
  - The source code is proprietary and not intended for public release
  - The FRE almost certainly does NOT apply

Do not rely on this exception without a detailed legal review.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PRACTICAL HR AND ACCESS CONTROL STEPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediate Actions (Before Any Source Code Access):

1. HALT SOURCE CODE ACCESS
   Remove or do not grant repository access to the employee until licence analysis
   is complete and a deemed export licence is obtained. This is a bright-line
   compliance requirement.

2. CONDUCT NATIONALITY VERIFICATION
   Obtain documentation confirming citizenship status. Under the most-restrictive
   nationality rule, Chinese nationality controls. Confirm there are no additional
   citizenships via an HR screening questionnaire (ensure questionnaire is
   legally reviewed for employment law compliance in your jurisdiction).

3. CLASSIFY ALL TECHNOLOGIES THE EMPLOYEE WILL ACCESS
   Map the specific source code modules, libraries, algorithms, and documentation
   the employee's role requires. Classify each under the EAR:
   - Encryption source code → 5D002 (primary)
   - Encryption technology (design specs, algorithm documentation) → 5E002
   - Test equipment → 5B002 (if applicable)

4. SCREEN THE EMPLOYEE AGAINST RESTRICTED PARTY LISTS
   Run the employee's name against:
   - BIS Entity List (Supplement 4, Part 744)
   - BIS Denied Persons List
   - BIS Unverified List
   - OFAC SDN List
   Use the Consolidated Screening List at trade.gov/consolidated-screening-list.
   Document the screening result. A match on any list creates a hard stop.

5. APPLY FOR DEEMED EXPORT LICENCE VIA SNAP-R
   File Form BIS-748P at snap-r.bis.doc.gov. Include:
   - Description of the 5D002 source code
   - Employee's name, nationality, job title, and role description
   - End-use statement: internal software development only
   - BIS-748P-A supplement for encryption items
   Engagement of export counsel experienced in encryption licences is strongly
   recommended given the NSA referral requirement.

6. IMPLEMENT ACCESS CONTROLS PENDING LICENCE
   During the period between hiring and licence receipt:
   - Assign the employee to non-controlled tasks (documentation, UI work, testing
     of non-encryption modules, or other EAR99 activities)
   - Use role-based access controls (RBAC) on all code repositories
   - Ensure the employee cannot access controlled source code through indirect
     means (shared drives, Slack/Teams file sharing, screen sharing, pair
     programming on controlled modules)

7. ESTABLISH ONGOING COMPLIANCE CONTROLS
   Once a licence is obtained:
   - Record the licence number, conditions, and expiry date
   - Document the access scope — what code repositories and modules are covered
   - Audit access logs periodically to ensure usage within licence scope
   - Re-apply at least 60 days before licence expiry
   - Update access controls if the employee's role changes

8. TRAINING
   Ensure the employee completes EAR/deemed export training before accessing any
   controlled material. Document completion. Retain training records for 5 years
   (§ 762.6).

9. RECORDKEEPING (§ 762)
   Retain all of the following for 5 years:
   - Deemed export licence application and approval
   - Nationality screening documentation
   - Restricted party screening records (dated)
   - Access control logs
   - Training records

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. BROADER COMPLIANCE PROGRAMME IMPLICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This scenario highlights gaps that should be addressed in your Export Compliance
Programme (ECP) if they are not already in place:

Element 3 — Written Policies and Procedures:
  Your ECP should include a written procedure for hiring foreign nationals into
  roles that involve access to controlled technology. The procedure should require:
  - Pre-hire deemed export screening for all technical roles
  - Classification review of technologies in the role scope
  - Licence or exception determination before access is granted
  - HR and Legal coordination on questionnaire design (nationality questions must
    comply with employment discrimination law, but may be asked for export
    compliance purposes under § 734.13)

Element 4 — Training:
  HR staff and engineering managers must understand deemed export concepts.
  Engineers should understand that sharing controlled source code with foreign
  national colleagues — even informally, in a code review or via a shared screen —
  can constitute an unlicensed deemed export.

Element 5 — Restricted Party Screening:
  Foreign national employees should be screened against restricted party lists
  at hire and periodically thereafter (recommended: annually, and whenever lists
  are materially updated).

Element 6 — Due Diligence:
  For employees from D:1 countries in sensitive technical roles, enhanced due
  diligence is appropriate. This does not mean discriminatory treatment but does
  mean earlier and more thorough export compliance analysis.
