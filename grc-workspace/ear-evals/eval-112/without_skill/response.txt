DEEMED EXPORT COMPLIANCE ANALYSIS
US Software Company — Chinese National Employee Access to 5D002 Encryption Source Code
Date of Analysis: 2026-05-07

==============================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
==============================================================================

Granting a Chinese national (even one holding Australian dual citizenship) access
to source code for a product classified under ECCN 5D002 very likely triggers a
deemed export obligation under the Export Administration Regulations (EAR). A
BIS licence or applicable licence exception will almost certainly be required
before access is granted. This is a high-risk scenario that warrants careful
legal review before proceeding.

==============================================================================
1. BACKGROUND: THE DEEMED EXPORT RULE
==============================================================================

Under the EAR (15 CFR Part 734), a "deemed export" occurs when controlled
technology or source code is released to a foreign national inside the United
States. "Release" includes:

  - Visual inspection of technology by foreign nationals
  - Oral exchanges of information
  - Applying technology abroad after release in the US
  - Granting access to systems, source code repositories, or technical data

The deemed export is treated as an export to the foreign national's country of
citizenship/nationality, NOT the country where the release occurs (15 CFR
734.13).

==============================================================================
2. ECCN 5D002 — WHAT IT COVERS
==============================================================================

ECCN 5D002 ("Information Security" — software) falls under Category 5, Part 2
of the Commerce Control List (CCL). It covers software specifically designed or
modified for encryption, cryptanalysis, or key management functions. Source code
for encryption products (particularly those implementing non-standard or strong
encryption algorithms) typically falls under 5D002.c.1.

Control Reasons for 5D002:
  - AT  (Anti-Terrorism)
  - EI  (Encryption Items — the primary control reason)
  - NS  (National Security)

The EI (Encryption Items) controls are the dominant concern here and carry
specific review and licensing requirements under Supplement No. 1 to Part 742
and the encryption review provisions of Part 740.

==============================================================================
3. DUAL NATIONALITY — THE CRITICAL RULE
==============================================================================

BIS policy on dual nationals is unambiguous and unfavourable to the employer
in this scenario:

RULE: For deemed export purposes, BIS uses the MOST RESTRICTIVE nationality.
When a person holds citizenship in two countries, the country that imposes
the most restrictive export controls applies.

  - China is subject to comprehensive EI controls and significant NS controls.
  - Australia is a close ally with favourable treatment under EAR (e.g., Country
    Group A:1 — Wassenaar Arrangement).

Because the employee holds Chinese citizenship, BIS treats the deemed export
as an export TO CHINA regardless of the Australian citizenship. The Australian
citizenship does not dilute, override, or eliminate the China-related controls.

This is explicitly addressed in BIS guidance and has been the subject of
enforcement actions. Employers cannot rely on an employee's secondary
(more favourable) nationality to avoid licence requirements tied to the
primary (more restrictive) nationality.

Key point: The employee's Australian passport does not help here.

==============================================================================
4. CHINA'S CONTROL STATUS UNDER THE EAR
==============================================================================

China is classified in the following Country Groups relevant to this analysis:

  - Country Group D:1 (National Security concerns)
  - Country Group D:5 (US Arms Embargo)
  - NOT in Country Group A:1 (Wassenaar) or A:5/A:6 (Five Eyes / close allies)

For ECCN 5D002 with EI controls, exports/deemed exports to China are subject
to licence requirements or must qualify under a specific licence exception.

China is NOT on the EAR Exclusion list (e.g., not Cuba, Iran, North Korea,
Syria — which are subject to comprehensive embargoes), but it is a controlled
destination for encryption technology under the EI regime.

==============================================================================
5. LICENCE EXCEPTIONS — CAN ANY APPLY?
==============================================================================

Several licence exceptions exist for encryption items. The most commonly used
are:

A. ENC (Encryption — 15 CFR 740.17)

  ENC is the primary licence exception for encryption technology. It has
  multiple tiers:

  ENC Section 740.17(b)(1) — Mass market encryption software:
    - Applies to products that qualify as "mass market" under the EAR
    - Source code for encryption is generally NOT eligible for mass market
      treatment under 740.17(b)(1). The exception is more readily available
      for object code / compiled products, not source code.
    - Source code for 5D002 products typically requires a higher tier review.

  ENC Section 740.17(b)(3) — Government end users (allies):
    - Does not apply here (employee is not a government end user).

  ENC Section 740.17(d) — Unrestricted encryption source code:
    - Covers source code that is publicly available (e.g., posted to the
      internet without access controls). If the source code has already been
      published openly, it may qualify. However, most proprietary company
      source code does NOT qualify here.

  Annual Self-Classification Report: Companies using ENC must file an annual
  encryption registration/self-classification report with BIS and NSA
  (Supplement No. 8 to Part 742). Failure to file is itself a violation.

B. TSR (Technology and Software — Unrestricted, 15 CFR 740.13)

  TSR allows release of technology and source code that is:
    - Released to the public domain (no restriction on further dissemination)
    - Or "educational" in nature (basic scientific information in public domain)

  Proprietary commercial source code is NOT eligible for TSR.

C. TMP (Temporary — 15 CFR 740.9)

  For temporary exports/releases. This would not be applicable to ongoing
  employment access.

D. NLR (No Licence Required)?

  For 5D002 with EI controls and a Chinese national, NLR is not available
  as a blanket matter. The EI controls require affirmative action (either
  a licence or a qualifying licence exception with proper procedures).

CONCLUSION ON EXCEPTIONS: Unless the source code qualifies as publicly
available/open source (in which case ENC 740.17(d) or TSR may apply after
review), a BIS licence will very likely be required for a Chinese national to
access proprietary 5D002 encryption source code.

==============================================================================
6. OPEN SOURCE / PUBLICLY AVAILABLE CARVE-OUT
==============================================================================

If your source code is truly publicly available (e.g., on a public GitHub repo
with no access controls), the EAR's "publicly available" exemption (15 CFR
734.7) may mean the technology is not subject to the EAR at all. In that case,
no deemed export licence would be required.

However, this is a narrow carve-out:
  - The code must be genuinely publicly available without restriction
  - Most commercial encryption source code is proprietary and does NOT qualify
  - Even if parts of the codebase use open-source libraries, the proprietary
    integration, configuration, and custom components remain controlled

Do not assume this applies without a thorough legal review of what is public
vs. proprietary.

==============================================================================
7. WHAT OBLIGATIONS DOES THE COMPANY HAVE?
==============================================================================

Step 1 — Classify the technology
  Confirm the ECCN 5D002 classification is accurate. If any doubt, request
  a commodity classification ruling from BIS (CCATS request).

Step 2 — Do not grant access until compliant
  Access to controlled source code must be withheld until either:
    (a) A BIS licence is obtained; or
    (b) An applicable licence exception is confirmed in writing by counsel; or
    (c) A legal determination is made that the technology is publicly available
        and outside EAR jurisdiction.

Step 3 — Encryption Self-Classification / Annual Report
  If not already filed, ensure your encryption products are registered under
  the annual encryption self-classification report (Supplement No. 8 to Part
  742). This is a standalone compliance obligation separate from the deemed
  export question.

Step 4 — Apply for a BIS Licence if required
  Submit a licence application to BIS via SNAP-R (the online portal).
  For an individual deemed export:
    - Include the employee's biographical information
    - Describe the technology (ECCN, technical parameters)
    - Describe the business need
    - Expect BIS review times of 30–90+ days (encryption items may take longer
      due to NSA referral under EAR Part 742.15)

Step 5 — Implement internal controls
  While the licence application is pending (or as ongoing compliance):
    - Restrict the employee's repository/code access by role or need-to-know
    - Maintain access logs
    - Train the employee and their manager on EAR obligations
    - Document your compliance analysis in writing

Step 6 — Consult export counsel
  Given the complexity (5D002, Chinese national, dual nationality), engage a
  qualified US export control attorney before making any access decisions.

==============================================================================
8. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
==============================================================================

EAR violations (including deemed export violations) carry serious consequences:

Civil penalties:
  - Up to $364,992 per violation (adjusted annually for inflation) or twice the
    value of the transaction, whichever is greater (as of recent adjustments)

Criminal penalties:
  - Up to $1,000,000 per violation and/or up to 20 years imprisonment
    (for wilful violations — Export Control Reform Act of 2018 / ECRA)

Other consequences:
  - Denial of export privileges (debarment from export activities)
  - Reputational damage
  - Government contract ineligibility

BIS has actively pursued deemed export enforcement cases. This is not a
theoretical risk.

==============================================================================
9. PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS — STRUCTURING FOR COMPLIANCE
==============================================================================

While the licence application is pending, consider whether the employee can
perform their role without accessing the controlled source code:
  - Can work be restructured to use compiled binaries only?
  - Can the employee work on non-encryption modules of the codebase?
  - Can access be granted to non-controlled supporting tools only?

If the employee's role genuinely requires source code access and a licence is
needed, plan for the lead time. BIS encryption licence applications involving
China can take several months.

Some companies obtain a blanket/standing deemed export licence for categories
of technology applicable to recurring employee situations, rather than
applying per-employee. Consider this for ongoing hiring needs.
