EAR COMPLIANCE ANALYSIS
RF Power Amplifier — 6 GHz, 40 W Output, Commercial Wireless Base Station Use
Export Destination: Germany

STEP 1 — JURISDICTION DETERMINATION (Order of Review, § 732.3)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Before classifying under the EAR, apply the mandatory Order of Review.

1a. USML Check (ITAR, 22 CFR Part 121)
    The item is a commercial RF power amplifier designed for civilian wireless
    base-station infrastructure (3GPP/commercial 5G or 4G). It is NOT specially
    designed for a military application. USML Category XI (Military Electronics)
    covers military radios and electronic warfare equipment; a commercial base-
    station PA that is not "specially designed" for military use does not fall
    there. CONCLUSION: Not on USML — ITAR jurisdiction does NOT apply.

1b. Other Agency Controls
    Not a nuclear item (NRC), pharmaceutical (FDA), or controlled substance
    (DEA). No other agency has exclusive jurisdiction.

1c. Subject to the EAR (§ 734.3)
    If the amplifier is of US origin (manufactured in the US) or physically
    located in the US, it is subject to the EAR. This analysis proceeds on
    that basis.

JURISDICTION RESULT: Item is subject to the EAR. Proceed to CCL classification.

STEP 2 — ECCN CLASSIFICATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The item's key technical parameters:
  • Frequency: 6 GHz
  • Output power: 40 W (continuous or peak — relevant to technical thresholds)
  • Function: RF power amplification
  • End-use: Commercial wireless base station (civilian infrastructure)

CCL Category: Category 3 — Electronics

The primary ECCN to evaluate is 3A001, which controls electronic components
including microwave and millimeter-wave components.

ECCN 3A001 — Analysis:

ECCN 3A001.b covers microwave solid-state amplifiers. Specifically,
3A001.b.4 controls "microwave solid-state amplifiers and microwave assemblies
or modules" with all of the following characteristics:
  (a) Designed for frequencies exceeding 2.7 GHz; AND
  (b) An "instantaneous bandwidth" of more than one octave; OR
      (c) A saturated power output of greater than certain thresholds.

For a 6 GHz amplifier, the frequency criterion (> 2.7 GHz) is clearly met.
The 40 W output power at 6 GHz is significant and likely exceeds the CCL
thresholds specified in 3A001.b.4 (which set out power/frequency combination
thresholds for amplifier assemblies).

Additionally, 3A001.b.2 controls "microwave power amplifiers" designed to
operate at frequencies exceeding 31.8 GHz — this item at 6 GHz falls below
that threshold, so .b.2 does not apply directly.

The most applicable subparagraph for a 6 GHz, 40 W RF power amplifier is
3A001.b.4 (microwave solid-state amplifiers operating above 2.7 GHz with
elevated saturated output power). However, the detailed thresholds in
3A001.b.4 depend on exact instantaneous bandwidth and saturated power levels
that should be confirmed against the current regulatory text.

ECCN CLASSIFICATION: 3A001 (most likely 3A001.b.4)

Reasons for Control under 3A001:
  • NS — National Security (Column 1)
  • AT — Anti-Terrorism (Column 1)

Note: 3A001 does NOT typically carry MT (Missile Technology) controls for
commercial PA components designed for civilian wireless infrastructure, but
you must confirm the item has no missile/guidance application and that it is
not "specially designed" for a missile or unmanned aerial vehicle system.

If upon review of the exact CCL technical parameters the item does NOT meet
the 3A001.b.4 thresholds (e.g., output power or bandwidth falls below the
controlled levels), then the item may instead classify as EAR99 — subject
to EAR but with no license requirement to most destinations absent end-use
or end-user concerns (see Step 5 below). Consider submitting a CCATS request
via SNAP-R (Form BIS-748P-A) to obtain a binding BIS classification if there
is any ambiguity.

Destination: Germany

Germany's Country Group Status:
  • Country Group A:1 (Wassenaar Arrangement member) — YES
  • Country Group A:5 (allied/partner, license-friendly) — YES
  • Germany is a NATO ally, EU member, and long-standing US partner
  • Germany is NOT in Country Group D or E

Country Chart lookup for ECCN 3A001 — NS Column 1 and AT Column 1:

  NS Column 1 / Germany: The Commerce Country Chart shows NO "X" in the NS
  Column 1 row for Germany. Germany, as an A:1/A:5 country and Wassenaar
  member, does not have an "X" in NS Column 1.

  AT Column 1 / Germany: Germany is not a state sponsor of terrorism and
  does not have an "X" in AT Column 1.

LICENSE REQUIREMENT RESULT: No license is required based on the Country
Chart analysis for a 3A001 item exported to Germany.

The transaction qualifies as "No License Required" (NLR) for this
ECCN/destination combination.

STEP 4 — LICENSE EXCEPTION ANALYSIS (Part 740)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Because the Country Chart analysis above shows no license is required for
Germany (NLR determination), a formal license exception is not needed to
authorize the export. However, note the following for completeness:

If for any reason a license were required, the most relevant exception would
be License Exception STA (Strategic Trade Authorization, § 740.20) —
applicable to exports to Country Group A:5 countries of items controlled for
NS reasons. Germany qualifies as an A:5 country. STA would authorize export
of 3A001 items to Germany subject to the conditions in § 740.20(b)(2),
including obtaining a STA eligibility statement from the consignee.

Other potentially applicable exceptions to note:
  • LVS (Limited Value Shipments, § 740.3): If the value of the shipment is
    below the LVS dollar threshold listed in the 3A001 entry, LVS may apply.
    Check the ECCN's LVS column for the applicable per-shipment value limit.
  • GBS (Group B Shipments, § 740.4): Applicable to NS-only items going to
    Country Group B. Germany is in A:1/A:5 (a more permissive grouping), so
    GBS may apply to NS-only controlled items — but again, NLR already
    applies here.

CONCLUSION FOR LICENSE EXCEPTIONS: Not required; the export qualifies as NLR.

STEP 5 — END-USER AND END-USE CONTROLS (Part 744)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Even with an NLR determination, you MUST conduct the following checks before
every shipment:

5a. Restricted Party Screening
    Screen all parties to the transaction — buyer, consignee, end-user,
    freight forwarder, bank, and any intermediate parties — against:

    (i)   Entity List (Supplement 4 to Part 744): A license is required for
          ALL items subject to EAR for any entity on this list. No exceptions
          available.
    (ii)  Denied Persons List (Part 764): Absolute prohibition. Do not
          proceed.
    (iii) Unverified List (Supplement 6 to Part 744): Cannot use license
          exceptions; must obtain UVL Statement before proceeding.
    (iv)  Military End-User (MEU) List (Supplement 7 to Part 744): License
          required for items in Supplement 2 to Part 744 if the party
          appears on this list.
    (v)   OFAC SDN List: Treasury-administered; full block.

    Use the Consolidated Screening List at trade.gov/consolidated-screening-list
    for a single-search check across BIS, State, and Treasury lists.

    Germany-based entities are generally low-risk, but screening is mandatory
    regardless of destination.

5b. WMD End-Use Prohibitions (§ 744.2–744.6)
    RF power amplifiers at 6 GHz could theoretically support radar or
    directed-energy applications. You must have no knowledge or reason to
    know that the amplifier will be used in:
      • Nuclear weapons development (§ 744.2)
      • Missile systems capable of delivering WMD (§ 744.3)
      • Chemical or biological weapons (§ 744.4)
    The stated end-use (commercial wireless base station) is a well-recognized
    civilian application. Document the end-use clearly.

5c. Red Flag Review (§ 732.6)
    Assess whether any "Red Flags" are present:
      • Is the buyer reluctant to provide end-use details? (Not typical for a
        licensed telecoms operator)
      • Does the order quantity, configuration, or payment method raise
        concerns?
      • Is the shipping route unusual for Germany?
    If red flags are identified, you must resolve them through due diligence
    before proceeding — no export until concerns are cleared.

STEP 6 — EXPORT CLEARANCE AND DOCUMENTATION (Part 758)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EEI Filing (Electronic Export Information):
  • EEI via AES (Automated Export System) is required for shipments valued
    over $2,500 per Schedule B number, or for any shipment requiring an
    export license (15 CFR § 30.37).
  • For a 40 W RF power amplifier valued above $2,500 (which is highly
    likely), file EEI via AES before export.
  • Enter the ECCN (3A001) and "NLR" as the export license code on the EEI
    filing.

Commercial Invoice and Shipper's Export Declaration:
  • Include ECCN classification and NLR designation on shipping documents.
  • Clearly state the end-use: "Commercial wireless base station — civilian
    telecommunications infrastructure."

STEP 7 — RECORDKEEPING (Part 762)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Retain all records for 5 years from the date of export:
  • Purchase orders and sales contracts
  • Commercial invoices and packing lists
  • Bills of lading / airway bills
  • AES/EEI filing confirmation
  • Restricted party screening records (date, lists checked, results)
  • End-use documentation from the consignee (recommended best practice)
  • Internal ECCN classification memorandum

Records must be available to BIS on request.

IMPORTANT CAVEATS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Self-classification carries legal responsibility. If you are uncertain
   whether the amplifier meets 3A001.b.4 thresholds — particularly regarding
   instantaneous bandwidth, saturated output power at 6 GHz, and gain — submit
   a CCATS request to BIS for an official, binding determination.

2. Dual-use nature: A 6 GHz, 40 W PA can have radar and electronic warfare
   applications. If the device is configurable for or marketed to military
   customers, revisit the USML (Category XI) and consider a Commodity
   Jurisdiction (CJ) request to DDTC.

3. German import controls: Germany applies EU dual-use Regulation (EU) 2021/821
   on the import/domestic transfer side. This analysis covers US export
   requirements only. Verify with your German consignee whether any EU or
   German national import permits are required for their end-use.

4. BIS regulations are updated frequently. Verify classifications and Country
   Chart entries against the current version of the EAR at ecfr.gov or
   bis.doc.gov before each shipment.
