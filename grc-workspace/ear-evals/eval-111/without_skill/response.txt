EAR CLASSIFICATION AND EXPORT LICENCE ANALYSIS
RF Power Amplifier — 6 GHz, 40 W Output, Commercial Wireless Base Station Use

Generated: 2026-05-07
Question: We manufacture an RF power amplifier that operates at 6 GHz with an output power of 40W. It is used in commercial wireless base stations. How do I classify it under the EAR, and does it need an export licence to ship to Germany?

================================================================================
1. OVERVIEW OF THE EAR CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

The Export Administration Regulations (EAR), administered by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) within the U.S. Department of Commerce, control the export of dual-use items (items with both commercial and potential military applications). Classification begins by determining whether the item is described by an Export Control Classification Number (ECCN) on the Commerce Control List (CCL), found at 15 C.F.R. Part 774, Supplement No. 1.

If an item is not described by any ECCN, it is classified as EAR99 — the lowest-control designation. EAR99 items may still require a licence in certain circumstances (e.g., prohibited end-use, prohibited end-user, embargoed destination), but they generally do not require a licence for export to close allies.

================================================================================
2. RELEVANT ECCN ANALYSIS
================================================================================

The item in question — an RF power amplifier operating at 6 GHz with 40 W output power for commercial wireless base stations — must be evaluated against the CCL, particularly Category 3 (Electronics) and relevant subcategories.

2.1 Primary Candidate: ECCN 3A001

ECCN 3A001 covers "Electronic components, as follows (see List of Items Controlled)." The most relevant sub-paragraph for this product is:

  3A001.b — "Radio-frequency (RF) power amplifiers" and related items.

Specifically, 3A001.b.1 and 3A001.b.2 address amplifiers designed or modified for operation above certain frequency and power thresholds. The precise control parameters under 3A001.b have historically included:

  - Frequency: Controls often engage for amplifiers capable of operating at frequencies associated with radar or electronic warfare bands, or above certain GHz thresholds specified in the technical note.
  - Output power: Thresholds vary by subparagraph and frequency band.
  - Designed/modified for: Military radar, electronic countermeasures (ECM), or specific wideband characteristics.

At 6 GHz and 40 W, the amplifier sits in the C-band / lower S-to-C-band range. Commercial wireless base station amplifiers (e.g., for LTE, 5G, or similar cellular infrastructure) operating in this range are generally purpose-built for commercial telecommunications. However, 6 GHz at 40 W approaches or may exceed certain control thresholds specified in 3A001.b depending on the exact regulatory language in force at the time of export.

2.2 Other Potentially Relevant ECCNs

  - 3A002: This covers general-purpose electronic equipment not specifically enumerated in 3A001. Less likely to apply here given that power amplifiers have explicit treatment in 3A001.
  - 5A001: Category 5, Part 1 (Telecommunications) covers telecommunications equipment and systems. RF components used in telecommunications end-use may also be reviewed under 5A001, though dedicated RF amplifier components are more typically captured under 3A001.
  - EAR99: If the amplifier's specifications fall below all ECCN thresholds — for example, if the power level and frequency combination does not meet the specific numeric parameters in 3A001.b — the item may qualify as EAR99.

2.3 Key Classification Considerations

To determine the exact ECCN with certainty, you should:

  a) Review the current text of ECCN 3A001.b in the CCL (15 C.F.R. Part 774, Supp. 1) for the precise frequency and power thresholds applicable at the time of export, as these parameters are periodically revised through the Wassenaar Arrangement and subsequent regulatory updates.

  b) Assess whether the amplifier is "specially designed" for any military, radar, or electronic warfare application — even if your stated use is commercial. The design intent and capability matter, not only the stated end-use.

  c) Consider whether the amplifier has wideband tuning capability, or whether it is narrowband and fixed-frequency for commercial cellular use (which tends to support a lower-control classification).

  d) If uncertain, submit a Commodity Classification Request (CCR) to BIS using BIS Form 748P, Supplement 1, for an authoritative ECCN determination. This is the safest path for borderline items.

Working assumption for this analysis: Given the commercial wireless base station end-use and 40 W at 6 GHz, the most likely classification is ECCN 3A001 (if the item meets control parameters) or EAR99 (if it falls below them). For the remainder of this analysis, both scenarios are addressed.

3. LICENCE REQUIREMENTS FOR EXPORT TO GERMANY
================================================================================

3.1 Germany as a Destination

Germany is a member of:
  - The European Union (EU)
  - NATO
  - The Wassenaar Arrangement on Export Controls for Conventional Arms and Dual-Use Goods and Technologies
  - The Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG)
  - The Australia Group
  - The Missile Technology Control Regime (MTCR)

For EAR purposes, Germany is in Country Group A:1 (Wassenaar Arrangement participants) and Country Group B. It is NOT in any of the more restrictive country groups (D:1 through D:5, or E:1/E:2). Germany is not subject to a U.S. embargo or comprehensive sanctions.

3.2 If the Amplifier is Classified EAR99

No licence is required to export to Germany. EAR99 items may be exported to Germany without a licence, subject only to the general prohibitions (Part 736) — i.e., you must still check denied parties lists and ensure no prohibited end-use or end-user is involved.

3.3 If the Amplifier is Classified ECCN 3A001

The Reasons for Control (RFC) associated with 3A001 typically include:
  - NS (National Security) — Column 1 or Column 2
  - AT (Anti-Terrorism) — Column 1
  - Possibly MT (Missile Technology) depending on sub-paragraph

For Germany and Country Group A:1/B destinations:

  - NS Column 1 controls: A licence exception is generally available. The most relevant is Licence Exception STA (Strategic Trade Authorization) under 15 C.F.R. § 740.20, which authorises export of many NS-controlled items to close allies including Germany without a specific licence, provided all STA conditions are met (e.g., the item is not 600-series/munitions, the consignee provides required STA assurances, etc.).

  - Alternatively, Licence Exception GOV may apply if the end-user is a government entity.

  - AT Column 1: Germany is not a terrorism-supporting state; AT controls do not restrict exports to Germany.

  - A specific licence (from BIS) would generally NOT be required for export to Germany for a commercial-use 3A001 amplifier, assuming STA or another applicable licence exception covers the transaction and no red flags are present.

3.4 Summary of Licence Determination

|----------------|--------------------|-------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| EAR99          | None               | No                      | Check denied parties, no other issues      |
| 3A001          | NS1, AT1           | Likely No               | Licence Exception STA typically available  |

In practice, for a commercial RF power amplifier destined for wireless base station infrastructure in Germany, a specific export licence is very unlikely to be required. The combination of Germany's country group status, the commercial end-use, and available licence exceptions (particularly STA) means most such shipments proceed without individual BIS licence approval.

================================================================================
4. MANDATORY COMPLIANCE STEPS (REGARDLESS OF LICENCE REQUIREMENT)
================================================================================

Even when no licence is required, U.S. exporters must:

  1. Classify the item accurately and document the classification rationale in your export compliance records.

  2. Screen all parties: Check the consignee, end-user, and any intermediaries against:
     - BIS Denied Persons List (DPL)
     - BIS Entity List
     - BIS Unverified List
     - OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
     - State Department Debarred List (ITAR)
     - EU and UN consolidated sanctions lists (for completeness)

  3. Obtain an End-User Statement or End-Use Certificate if the item is ECCN-controlled and the transaction warrants it (good practice even when not strictly required).

  4. Comply with Electronic Export Information (EEI) filing requirements via AES (Automated Export System) / ACE if the shipment value exceeds $2,500 per Schedule B number or if a licence is required.

  5. Retain export records for five years from the date of export (15 C.F.R. § 762.6).

  6. Ensure your shipping documents (commercial invoice, packing list, shipper's export declaration) accurately describe the item and its ECCN/EAR99 classification.

================================================================================
5. IMPORTANT CAVEATS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

  a) This analysis is based on general knowledge of EAR regulations and is not a substitute for legal advice from a licensed export control attorney or a formal BIS commodity classification ruling.

  b) Export control regulations change. The specific numeric thresholds in ECCN 3A001.b should be verified against the current CCL text at the time of each export.

  c) If your amplifier has any specifications or design features that approach military-grade performance (e.g., broad tuning range, rugged MIL-SPEC construction, high efficiency at wide bandwidths), the classification risk increases and professional legal review is strongly advised.

  d) Germany has its own export controls under the EU Dual-Use Regulation (EU) 2021/821 and national Aussenwirtschaftsgesetz (AWG) / Aussenwirtschaftsverordnung (AWV). Your German customer or distributor may need to comply with EU-side re-export controls, particularly for onward shipment of the amplifier to third countries.

  e) If the amplifier were to be used in any application that could support military radar, electronic intelligence (ELINT), or electronic warfare — even incidentally — the risk profile changes significantly, potentially triggering ITAR jurisdiction (if it meets USML Category XI parameters) or requiring a specific BIS licence even for allied destinations.

================================================================================
6. RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
================================================================================

  1. Pull the current CCL text for ECCN 3A001.b and compare your amplifier's exact specifications (frequency range, output power, gain, bandwidth, efficiency) against each control parameter.

  2. If the product clearly falls below all 3A001.b thresholds, document it as EAR99 with a written classification rationale.

  3. If the product meets or is borderline on any 3A001.b parameter, consider filing a Commodity Classification Request (CCR) with BIS for a binding determination.

  4. Screen all parties in the Germany transaction against current denied/restricted party lists.

  5. Determine whether Licence Exception STA (or another exception) applies and document that determination.

  6. File EEI via ACE/AES if required.

  7. Consult an export control attorney or a licensed Export Management Company (EMC) if your product line includes multiple RF components with varying specifications, to establish a scalable classification process.

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

An RF power amplifier operating at 6 GHz with 40 W output power for commercial wireless base stations most likely falls under ECCN 3A001 (electronics — RF power amplifiers) or potentially EAR99 depending on whether its specifications meet the current CCL control thresholds. For export to Germany — a Wassenaar Arrangement member and close U.S. ally — a specific BIS export licence is very unlikely to be required. Licence Exception STA or other applicable exceptions typically authorise such shipments. However, accurate self-classification, denied-party screening, and proper record-keeping are mandatory regardless of whether a licence is needed.
